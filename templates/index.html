Grad-Cam

Need of this project? Although neural networks prove to be useful, especially in such tasks of health-care sector, the “WHY” of their functionality is a mystery to a great extent. Techniques like Grad-CAM offer a chance to peek into the “black-box” and gain insights around the inner workings of these models. It not only serves as a validation as to whether the CNN is picking up on the right features contributing to the produced result, but may also turn out to provide intuitions about the features that the human-counterpart should consider while making judgements. What is this page and project about? This page is an effort towards the interpretability of Convolution Neural Networks through Grad-CAMs. In this project, we trained a transfer-learned image classifier to classify them according to the most likely skin-disease the image might correspond to. Further, by manifesting the technique of Grad-CAMs on feature-maps, the results (using heat-maps) for some sample images are shown here. One can also test their own images on the classifier and produce the respective Grad-CAMs by submitting the images below. What is Grad-CAM? Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM), uses the gradients of any target concept, flowing into the final convolutional layer to produce a coarse localization map highlighting important regions in the image for predicting the concept. This technique is referred from the paper Grad-CAM: Visual Explanations from Deep Networks via Gradient-based Localization (7 Oct 2016)
